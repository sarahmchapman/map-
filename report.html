How to read your astrocartography lines

Not every line on your map carries the same weight. Here is how the planets in your chart shape what each line offers — places where things flow, places where things shift, and places where the work runs deep.

How this works

Your birth chart is the same wherever you go — but at every spot on Earth, different planets sit in different positions overhead. The lines on your map mark where each planet sits directly above the horizon, below it, or rising and setting on the eastern and western edges. Stand near a line, and that planet's themes get louder.

But how those themes feel when they get louder depends on how that planet is doing in your chart to begin with. A planet that is comfortable, supported, and in its element will amplify in a way that feels generative. A planet that is uncomfortable or under pressure will amplify those same conditions. The categories below sort your planets by the kind of amplification their lines are likely to bring.

Harmonious lines

Being near a line of these planets is strongly in your favour. The planet becomes amplified in a positive way — these are places where the themes that planet rules tend to flow.

Dynamic lines

The planets behind these lines have some things working in their favour and some working against. What gets amplified is a mix — some themes come through cleanly, others bring friction.

Challenging lines

These lines show areas of high friction. The planet is amplified, but because it lacks support in your natal chart, you will experience the more difficult or demanding side of its themes. These are also where the deepest growth tends to happen.
